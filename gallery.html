Team 1

The IOC Refugee Olympic Team represents more than 100 million forcibly displaced people worldwide. Made up of 37 athletes, hosted by 15 National Olympic Committees and competing across 12 sports, the team demonstrates to the world that refugees are an enrichment to the society.

Team 2

In October 2018, with originally two years to go until the Olympic Games Tokyo 2020, the IOC Session decided that there would be an IOC Refugee Olympic Team for Tokyo 2020, and entrusted Olympic Solidarity with the task of establishing the conditions for participation and the identification and selection process for a team in Tokyo..

IOC Refugee Olympic Team 2016: As part of its commitment to aiding potential elite athletes affected by the worldwide refugee crisis, the IOC asked National Olympic Committees (NOCs) around the world to identify any refugee athletes with the potential to qualify for the Olympic Games Rio 2016. Such candidates could then receive funding from Olympic Solidarity to assist with their preparations and qualification efforts.
