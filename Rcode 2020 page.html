Record of Olympics 2020

Pyeongchang, South Korea - February 9 to 25, 2020

The 2020 Winter Olympics, officially known as the XXIII Olympic Winter Games, were a major international multi-sport event held in Pyeongchang, South Korea, from February 9 to 25, 2020. The games featured a record 102 events in 15 sports, with over 2,900 athletes from 92 countries participating.

Summary of the Games

The 2020 Olympics were marked by several historic moments, including the debut of big air snowboarding and mass start speed skating. The games also saw the introduction of mixed doubles curling and mixed team alpine skiing.

Norway dominated the medal table, winning a total of 39 medals, including 14 gold medals. Germany finished second with 31 medals, while Canada finished third with 29 medals.

Some of the standout performances of the games included:

  • Ester Ledecka (CZE) winning gold medals in both alpine skiing and snowboarding
  • Martin Fourcade (FRA) winning five gold medals in biathlon
  • Tessa Virtue and Scott Moir (CAN) winning gold medals in figure skating
